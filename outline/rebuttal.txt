The central result of Section 6 is Theorem 6.4, which establishes that the power series constitutes part of the minimal solution to a finite system of fixed-point equations. Given that the context is a semiring, this characterization of such power series as algebraic is natural, as discussed in paragraph 5; analogous definitions are well-established in formal language theory (e.g., Kuich and Salomaa, Semirings, Automata, Languages; Droste and Kuich, Handbook of Finite Automata) as well as in combinatorics (Banderier and Drmota, Formulae and Asymptotics for Coefficients of Algebraic Functions).
Corollary 6.5.1—which states that these series are also algebraic in the traditional field-theoretic sense—is an auxiliary result rather than the primary focus. This corollary could be significant for further decidability results (for instance, determining whether two PHORS share the same generating function), it is not our main concern. In contrast to the approach taken by Ong, the fixed-point system derived here defines power series with a distinct probabilistic interpretation. Consequently, as you correctly reamrked, the moments can be obtained by differentiating the power series. Nevertheless, even in this framework, the fixed-point characterization remains an indispensable.
